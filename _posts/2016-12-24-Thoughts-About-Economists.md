I've been doing some thinking lately about the failures of modern economics, specifically Federal Reserve Policy, and read an article [Economists versus the Economy](https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/mathematical-economics-training-too-narrow-by-robert-skidelsky-2016-12?utm_source=project-syndicate.org&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=authnote) that highlights something I have noticed implicitly in my studies: some of the world's most successful economists have did not have formal training in economics.
